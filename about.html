About

Etoile Dance Company was founded in 2012 through a passion for making dance accessible to everyone. Historically, dance has always been an expensive endeavor that few can afford. With research showing that dance has a plethora of physical and mental benefits, including but not limited to increased muscle strength, improved cardiovascular health, and higher level of confidence, the founder of Etoile Dance Company wanted to bring these benefits to all members of the community. Etoile Dance Company works with each individual's budget and welcomes everyone into its safe space that allows freedom of individual expression. Etoile Dance Company offers a variety of different classes, including ballet, contemporary, hip hop, jazz, and yoga to provide something for everyone. Please check out our classes and contact us today!

Our Mission

Embrace inclusion and access for all dancers.

Provide a safe space that encourages diversity, equity, and equality.

Create a long-lasting love for the art of dance by promoting creativity and individuality.

Our Team

Diane Chen

Founder/Artistic Director

Diane Chen is the founder and artistic director of Etoile Dance Company. Diane was a lead, professional dancer at the Oregon Ballet Theatre for over 15 years. Originally from China, she learned the art of dance in a small village in the Yunnan Province. Her dance lessons took place on the cobblestones outside her dance instructor's house. When she was 12 years old, Diane's parents decided to make a big change and moved to the United States. Diane knew that that was her chance to become a professional dancer and despite being a disadvantaged first generation immigrant, she fought for her dream. She now wants to pass her dream on to other aspiring dancers.

“I hope to make dancing accessible to anyone and everyone. Dance has a powerful strength to reshape lives and I want everyone to experience that joy.”


Amari Ibarra

Instructor

(modern, jazz, show-dance)

Amari was born and raised in San Francisco, California. She started her dancing career at the age of 3 years old. At age 10, she joined the San Francisco Dance Company, where she started her professional training as a dancer. Amari met Diane at a summer intensive program at the New York City Ballet when they were only 17 years old. When Diane decided to open her own dance studio, Amari was quick to move to Portland, Oregon to switch her career from a professional dancer to a dance instructor.

“Giving students confidence and self-esteem through dance is one of my favorite things about being an instructor. Come join my classes and let's boost that ego baby!”


Emily Breiman

Instructor

(ballet, jazz, show-dance, children's dance)

Emily is a native Oregonian. She was born and raised in Bend; a city surrounded by mountains. Emily was trained in classical ballet from the age of 6. In high school, she joined the dance team and quickly fell in love with jazz and show-dance. While getting her undergraduate degree in performing arts at the University of Oregon, she coached the high school varsity dance team at Rockfield High School and helped them win the state championships three years in a row. After graduating college, she moved to Portland, Oregon and Diane quickly welcomed her to her team.

“I love the emotions one can express through dance. The right facial expressions and movements can express so much sadness, joy, or anger. I'm excited to teach others the art.”


Gabby Winters

Instructor

(ballet, contemporary, yoga)

Gabby grew up in Anchorage, Alaska. She started her dance career at the Anchorage Ballet Academy, where she was spotted by a recruiter from the New York City Ballet. After graduating high school, she moved to New York to fulfill her dream of becoming a professional ballerina. During her time there, Gabby met Diane and moved to Oregon when Diane opened her own dance studio.

“No choreography will ever look the same performed by different people. I see the uniqueness in every individual and love how everyone can bring their own uniqueness into dance.”


James Moreland

Instructor

(hip hop, show-dance)

James started his dance career when his friends jokingly dared him to join the high school dance team. Not only did James make the varsity team his freshman year, but he fell in love with dance. After high school, he joined the Bello Dance Company and traveled the world to perform in the show “Rock the Ballet,” a hip show that combines ballet, hip hop, and modern jazz into a funky performance. After traveling the world, he was asked to participate in “Dancing with the Stars,” where he helped Selena Gomez win season 5. Retiring from the hectic lifestyle, he decided to move to the green Oregon.

“Don't tell the others that I am the best dance instructor in the world! My classes are definitely the most fun.”


Kenji Lee

Instructor

(ballet, modern, contemporary, hip hop)

Kenji grew up in Richmond, Virginia and started his dance at the young age of 3. He trained at the Richmond School of Dance in his youth and moved to Paris, France after graduating high school. There, he joined the Paris Opera Ballet and enjoyed life as a professional dancer for over 10 years. He and his wife met in Paris and decided to move to Oregon, where Kenji started his career as a choreographer at the Oregon Ballet Theatre. After meeting Diane, he decided to change to teaching, something he has always been passionate about.

“Dance is not about following every step perfectly, but about making it your own. One is a great performer when one is able to find the balance between following the choreography and making the dance personal.”

Tyler Richer

Instructor

(show-dance)
